Calibration, Not Alignment

We use the term calibration intentionally. We do not seek premature alignment. Alignment implies smoothing away differences, creating early consensus, or forcing uniformity where none yet exists. That is not what early-stage work requires.

Calibration is about synchronizing understanding — an honest mapping of starting conditions. It surfaces tensions, reveals misalignments, and exposes gaps — not to fix them immediately, but to understand them clearly.

What Calibration Surfaces

The real mission for the work — what success actually looks like
The intractable challenges keeping the CEO up at night
Current capabilities and constraints of the executive team
CEO expectations — and whether they match reality
Team readiness for value-creating challenges

Calibration Timeline

P1
Days 1 – 10

CEO Partnership

▼

Establishing a genuine partnership with the CEO. Articulating the mission, defining what value creation means, and identifying which challenges the team must take on first.

Initial CEO Conversation

Day 1-2

Deep conversation with the CEO to understand their vision, concerns, and expectations for the executive team. This often reveals that the CEO's own thinking is still forming — which is exactly why this partnership matters.

  • What does success look like for this team?
  • What are the existential challenges facing the organization?
  • What has the CEO inherited vs. what do they want to build?
Output: Initial mission framing and challenge identification

Define Value Creation Goals

Day 3-5

Work with the CEO to articulate what value creation will mean for this specific organization. Not generic strategy — specific, contextual outcomes that would shift markets or earn stakeholder confidence.

  • What outcomes would be transformational?
  • Which challenges are truly enterprise-critical?
  • Where should the team focus first?
Output: Value creation definition and priority challenge shortlist

Engagement Design

Day 5-10

Co-design the calibration approach with the CEO. Agree on how to introduce the work to the team, schedule observations and conversations, and begin shaping the calibration event.

  • How will the coach be introduced to the team?
  • What existing team meetings can be observed?
  • Timeline and logistics for individual conversations
  • Initial thinking on calibration event design
Output: Calibration plan and communication approach
P2
Days 10 – 35

Team Observation & Individual Conversations

▼

Understanding the relational, structural, and behavioral patterns at play. Meeting each executive to understand their view, their challenges, and the divergences that exist beneath the surface.

Team Observation

Day 10-20

The coach observes the team in their natural setting — existing meetings, decision forums, and interactions. Understanding what really happens, not what the org chart says should happen.

  • What gets discussed? What gets avoided?
  • Who speaks? Who doesn't?
  • How are decisions actually made?
  • What are the relational and power dynamics?
  • Where is energy? Where is tension?
Output: Observation notes on team dynamics, decision patterns, and behavioral norms

Individual Conversations

Day 15-30

One-on-one sessions with each executive. These conversations often reveal significant divergence — people are seeing different realities, solving for different problems, and operating with different assumptions.

  • Their view of the team and its effectiveness
  • The challenges they see facing the organization
  • Their aspirations and concerns
  • Their sense of what the work ahead requires
  • Where they feel aligned — and where they don't
Output: Individual perspective maps revealing convergence and divergence across the team

Synthesis & Pattern Recognition

Day 28-35

Integrate observations and conversations into a coherent picture. Identify the themes, tensions, and opportunities that will shape the calibration event. Share emerging insights with the CEO.

  • What patterns emerge across individual conversations?
  • Where are the critical misalignments?
  • What does the team not see about itself?
  • What is the team's readiness level for each of the five plays?
Output: Calibration synthesis document and CEO debrief
P3
Days 35 – 45

Calibration Event Design

▼

Designing a multi-day immersive session based on everything learned. The calibration event is the formal launch of the Value Creating Teams approach.

Event Architecture

Day 35-40

Design the structure, flow, and content of the calibration session in partnership with the CEO. Every element is tailored to what the synthesis revealed about this specific team.

  • Select the existential challenge the team will prioritize
  • Design exercises that introduce early versions of the five plays
  • Plan for surfacing divergence productively (not suppressing it)
  • Build in reflection and real-time adaptation
Output: Detailed calibration event agenda and facilitation plan

CEO Alignment on Design

Day 40-42

Walk the CEO through the event design. Ensure they understand the arc, are prepared for what may surface, and are ready to model the behaviors the work requires.

  • CEO role during the calibration event
  • Potential difficult moments and how to navigate them
  • Success criteria for the event
Output: CEO-aligned event plan and pre-brief

Logistics & Preparation

Day 42-45

Finalize all logistics. Prepare materials, pre-reads, and any inputs the team needs. Coordinate with chief of staff or executive assistant on venue, schedule, and communications.

Output: All materials prepared and logistics confirmed
P4
Days 45 – 50

The Calibration Event

▼

A multi-day immersive working session. Not conceptual training — a visceral experience of Value Creating Teams in practice. The team begins running the five plays for real.

Sensemaking: Shared View of Reality

Event Day 1

The team builds a shared (not identical) view of current reality — often discovering they've been looking at different versions of the same organization.

  • FBSN Exercise: Separating facts from beliefs, signals from noise
  • Surface the divergences revealed in individual conversations
  • Begin constructing a shared understanding of the landscape
Output: Shared reality map and FBSN matrix for priority challenge

Imagining: Stretching Shared Ambition

Event Day 1-2

Push beyond what feels realistic to explore what might be possible. Articulate a shared ambition that energizes and challenges the team.

  • Move from "what is" to "what could be"
  • Define an ambition that is both aspirational and grounded
  • Test the team's willingness to stretch
Output: Draft shared ambition statement

Navigating: First Experiments

Event Day 2

Experiment with navigating uncertainty together. What rhythm feels right? What decisions need to be made now? What can wait? Begin developing the muscle for real-time adaptation.

  • Test decision-making under uncertainty
  • Explore what rhythm the work demands
  • Identify early experiments and learning opportunities
Output: Initial navigation plan and experiment design

Collaborating: Reflecting on How We Work

Event Day 2-3

Reflect on collaboration patterns that emerged during the event itself. What worked? What didn't? Begin articulating the team's distinctive way of working.

  • What collaboration patterns emerged naturally?
  • Where did the team get stuck?
  • What norms does this team need?
Output: Early collaboration insights and working agreements

Closing: Commitments & Next Steps

Event Day 3

Lock in commitments. The team leaves with clarity on the existential challenge they will prioritize, the ambition they are pursuing, and the immediate next steps. This sets the stage for scaffolding.

  • Confirm the priority existential challenge
  • Agree on immediate actions and owners
  • Set expectations for cadence going forward
  • Individual reflections on what shifted
Output: Team commitment document and transition brief for scaffolding phase

What Comes Next: Designing Scaffolding

After the calibration event, the work transitions into the scaffolding phase. In partnership with the CEO, we design a custom architecture built around what this team needs to accomplish and how they need to grow. This includes:

Team design — Is this the right configuration for the challenges ahead?
Challenge selection — Which problems build the most muscle?
Rhythm — What frequency and depth of interactions does the team need?
Environment — What meeting structures and decision forums reinforce VCT behaviors?
Individual coaching — Supporting key leaders to develop inner capacities
Chief of Staff enablement — Managing day-to-day team mechanisms

The timeline varies — typically six to eighteen months of intensive work, followed by periodic support. The measure of success is always the same: can this team now create value on their own?
