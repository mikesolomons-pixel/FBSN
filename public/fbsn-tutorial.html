Facts, Beliefs, Signals, Noise

A structured practice for making strategic assumptions visible and turning collective insight into action.

1

Facts vs. Beliefs

The first axis of the FBSN framework asks: How certain are we? Every perspective a team holds falls somewhere on a spectrum from verified fact to untested belief. Most teams don't distinguish between the two, and that's where trouble starts.

Facts

Stable data points grounded in evidence. Facts inform today's performance and provide the foundation for sound decisions. But beware: failure happens when we stop questioning facts.

Beliefs

Hypotheses, assumptions, or convictions that haven't been fully verified. Beliefs shape tomorrow's strategy. Innovation happens when we convert beliefs into facts before competitors do.

Effective teams know how to differentiate between facts and beliefs — and value both. Facts inform today. Beliefs shape tomorrow.
2

Signals vs. Noise

The second axis asks: How important is this? Not everything that is true matters. Not every belief deserves attention. Signals are the perspectives that drive growth, transformation, or risk. Noise pulls teams off course.

Signals

High-importance inputs that demand focus. Signals help teams concentrate on what matters most. Speed comes from relentless prioritization — focusing on signals and ignoring distractions.

Noise

Low-importance inputs that pull teams off course. Individual perspectives outside of a broader context can be noisy, leading to chasing shiny objects. True but irrelevant. Interesting but unproven.

Effective teams know how to focus on the right signals and filter out the noise. They move faster by aligning around what's meaningful and letting go of what's distracting.
3

The Strategic Matrix

When you combine both axes, you get a powerful 2×2 matrix that makes strategic assumptions visible and helps teams decide where to focus.

Signal (Important)
Noise (Unimportant)
Fact (Certain)

Facts + Signals

Growth Drivers. Proven insights that directly accelerate growth. These are the data-backed levers to scale. Align for execution and impact.

Facts + Noise

Stable but Non-Differentiating. True but obvious. Useful for monitoring, not for winning. Eliminate operational drag.

Belief (Uncertain)

Beliefs + Signals

Strategic Bets. Promising hypotheses about where the next wave of growth could come from. Worth testing fast. Cultivate for discovery and growth.

Beliefs + Noise

Distracting Assumptions. Ideas or assumptions that sound exciting but don't tie clearly to value creation. Block off dead ends.

4

Example: Netflix at a Strategic Crossroads

In the late 2000s, Netflix faced a major inflection point. DVD-by-mail was still profitable, but growth was slowing. Consumer behavior was shifting toward on-demand digital content. Broadband access was expanding rapidly, and competitors were entering the streaming space.

Netflix had to make bold strategic bets — deciding what to scale, what to explore, and what to leave behind. The stakes: maintain relevance or risk becoming obsolete.

Imagine you're on the Netflix executive committee in 2008. You're hearing a range of perspectives in a strategy meeting. Some are data-driven, some are speculative. Which deserve attention — and which don't?

6

From Strategic Clarity to Action

The matrix doesn't end at classification. Each quadrant drives a different type of strategic decision: Must Do, Might Do, or Won't Do.

Must Do

Act now

Facts + Signals drive Must Do. High confidence, clear ROI, critical to short-term goals. Align execution, move fast, act with confidence.

Might Do

Test and learn

Beliefs + Signals drive Might Do. Promising but uncertain. Design experiments to validate. If proven, move to Must Do. If not, move to Won't Do.

Won't Do

Avoid distractions

Facts + Noise and Beliefs + Noise drive Won't Do. Avoid unnecessary distractions. Free organizational cognitive load. Revisit only if major changes occur.

7

Netflix: Turning Insight into Action

Must Do

Build and scale streaming

Streaming offers speed, convenience, and aligns with emerging consumer expectations. Invest in streaming technology, user experience, and content delivery to replace DVD-by-mail. This is a clear signal supported by consumer demand, tech trends, and operational efficiencies.

Might Do

Develop original programming

Owning content could differentiate Netflix and reduce dependency on third-party studios. Pilot original content and pursue selective exclusivity deals. This is a promising but unproven belief — it could reshape the market if successful.

Won't Do

Expand DVD operations

DVDs are profitable now, but the model is limited in scalability and future relevance. Phase out investment in physical distribution. Though true (fact), this is noise in the long-term growth strategy — it diverts attention and resources.

This is how Netflix balanced performance (streaming execution) with transformation (original content bets) — a clear example of turning strategic clarity into bold action.
8

Turning Insights into Action Plans

Once you've classified your perspectives and determined what to do, each category demands a different kind of planning:

Must Do

Plan for execution

What specific outcome defines success? Who owns it? What resources are needed? What's the timeline? What decisions are overdue because we already have enough confidence?

Might Do

Design an experiment

What assumption are you testing? How will you run the experiment? How will you gather and interpret feedback? What capabilities should we be ready to trigger?

Won't Do

Plan for communication

How will we communicate and enforce this decision? Does it impact specific groups? Will anyone need time to adjust? Under what conditions should we revisit?
