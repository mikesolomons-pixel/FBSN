How the Team Works Together

The first three plays describe what value-creating teams do. Collaborating describes how they do it together. This is where the team develops its distinctive way of working — the patterns, rhythms, and practices that become "how we work here."

Like a musical ensemble that practices structured compositions to enable improvisation, teams build their collaborative capacity through deliberate practice. But there is a critical distinction: teams should not write down norms that are not already lived. Real norms emerge organically from experience.

Helps teams see themselves clearly — hidden assumptions, personal histories, values that anchor perspectives
Continuous reflection woven into the team's rhythm, not quarterly off-sites
The team's operating system: refining how they run plays, adapting to new challenges, building a distinctive identity
Collaborating is where the team becomes more than the sum of its members
