Principles & Beliefs

Work in Progress

These principles and beliefs are evolving. They represent our current thinking about what makes executive teams capable of creating extraordinary value — shaped by experience, research, and ongoing conversation.

1

Leadership is a Team Sport

The most critical unit of leadership in any organization is the top team, not the individual at the top.

2

Value Creation Means Better Lives

Not just shareholder returns. Teams exist to create value across all stakeholders. If we're honest, most organizations are falling short.

3

Plays, Not Prescriptions

Like a sports team, executive teams need a playbook of adaptable moves, not rigid processes.

4

Scaffolding, Not Dependency

The goal is to build the team's own capability, not create reliance on external advisors.

5

Calibration Before Action

Invest in deeply understanding the team's context before prescribing solutions.

6

Practice Makes Permanent

Real change comes through repeated practice, not one-off events or workshops.

7

The CEO Partnership

Effective team transformation requires a genuine partnership with the CEO as co-architect of change.

8

Personal and Professional are Inseparable

Who you are shapes how you lead. The whole person shows up — and that's what makes the work real.

9

Conflict is Data, Not Dysfunction

Divergent views tell us where reality is unclear and where assumptions differ. The goal isn't consensus — it's shared clarity.

10

Bring Love to What You Do

The best teams are driven not just by strategy but by genuine care — for each other, for their people, and for the impact they have on the world.
