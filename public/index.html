Sensemaking — Understanding Where We Are

Before a team can chart a course forward, they need shared clarity about the current landscape. Sensemaking practices help teams distinguish verified facts from assumptions, and meaningful signals from background noise. This shared understanding becomes the foundation for every strategic decision that follows.

Play 5

Value Creating — Bringing It All Together

The final play integrates everything that comes before. Value Creating is where the team produces outcomes that only this team, in this moment, with this practice, could produce. It's not a destination — it's the continuous practice of turning learning into impact. Value creation is always contextual: specific to the domain, the problem, and the ambition of the organization.

After significant value creation, the cycle begins anew. Teams sense the changed landscape, imagine new possibilities, navigate the next set of unknowns, collaborate with deepened trust, and create value again. This is why Value Creating is a play, not an outcome — a repeatable capability the team builds and refines over time.

Three Horizons Framework

Horizon 1

Deliver Today

Managing the current business. Optimizing what exists, meeting commitments, and maintaining performance in the core.

Horizon 2

Build Tomorrow

Developing emerging opportunities. Investing in adjacencies and new capabilities that will drive future growth.

Horizon 3

Create the Future

Imagining transformational possibilities. Seeding ideas that may redefine the business and its role in the world.

Seven Big Ideas

1

Leadership is a Team Sport

The most critical unit of leadership in any organization is the top team, not the individual at the top.

2

Value Creation is the Mandate

Teams exist to create value that no individual or subset could create alone.

3

Plays, Not Prescriptions

Like a sports team, executive teams need a playbook of adaptable moves, not rigid processes.

4

Scaffolding, Not Dependency

The goal is to build the team's own capability, not create reliance on external advisors.

5

Calibration Before Action

Invest in deeply understanding the team's context before prescribing solutions.

6

Practice Makes Permanent

Real change comes through repeated practice, not one-off events or workshops.

7

The CEO Partnership

Effective team transformation requires a genuine partnership with the CEO as co-architect of change.
