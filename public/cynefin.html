The Cynefin Framework

Developed by Dave Snowden, Cynefin helps leaders understand the nature of the challenges they face and choose the right approach. Different domains demand fundamentally different responses.

Complex

Probe → Sense → Respond

Cause and effect only visible in retrospect. Use safe-to-fail experiments. Emergent practice.

Complicated

Sense → Analyze → Respond

Cause and effect discoverable through expertise. Multiple right answers exist. Good practice.

Chaotic

Act → Sense → Respond

No cause and effect relationship at system level. Act first to establish order. Novel practice.

Clear

Sense → Categorize → Respond

Cause and effect is obvious. Best practices exist and should be applied. Established practice.

Confused / Disorder

The state of not knowing which domain you are in

Explore & Act

Each domain requires a different response. Select a domain tab to explore your challenges and take the appropriate action.

● Clear Domain

These challenges have obvious cause-and-effect relationships. Best practices exist. The approach is: Sense → Categorize → Respond. Apply established best practice.

● Complicated Domain

These challenges require expert analysis. The relationship between cause and effect exists but isn't self-evident. The approach is: Sense → Analyze → Respond. There may be multiple right answers.

● Complex Domain

These challenges are unpredictable. Cause and effect can only be understood in retrospect. The approach is: Probe → Sense → Respond. Design safe-to-fail experiments to learn about the system.

● Chaotic Domain

These challenges are in crisis. There is no discernible cause-and-effect relationship. The approach is: Act → Sense → Respond. Take immediate action to establish order, then sense where stability lies.

Immediate Action Required

In the Chaotic domain, there is no time for analysis or experimentation. Act decisively to stabilize the situation. Once order is established, the challenge may shift into the Complex or Complicated domain where more deliberate approaches become possible.

● Confused / Disorder

These challenges haven't been clearly classified yet. The first step is to break them down or gather more information so they can be moved into one of the four primary domains.
